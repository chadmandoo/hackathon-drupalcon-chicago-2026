# Permission to Run Official Contest Rules

DrupalCon Chicago 2026  
Sponsored by Acquia Inc.

**NO PURCHASE NECESSARY TO ENTER OR WIN. A PURCHASE WILL NOT INCREASE YOUR CHANCES OF WINNING. VOID WHERE PROHIBITED BY LAW. THIS IS A SKILL-BASED CONTEST, NOT A RANDOM DRAWING. ODDS OF WINNING DEPEND ON THE NUMBER AND QUALITY OF ELIGIBLE ENTRIES RECEIVED.**

## 1. Event Overview

Permission to Run is an Acquia-sponsored hackathon held in conjunction with DrupalCon Chicago 2026. The contest is focused on Agent Experience (AX) in Drupal. Submissions are judged on merit using the published rubric in [`judging-rubric.md`](./judging-rubric.md). Acquia reserves the right to interpret these rules in its sole discretion.

## 2. Sponsor and Administrator

Sponsor and Administrator: Acquia Inc.  
Mailing Address: 53 State Street, 10th Floor, Boston, MA 02109  
Contact Email: hackathon@acquia.com

## 3. Entry Period

The contest opens on Friday, March 20, 2026 at 12:05 PM Central Time and closes on Wednesday, March 25, 2026 at 12:00 PM Central Time (the "Submission Deadline"). Entries submitted after the Submission Deadline will not be eligible for prizes. Judging will occur after the Submission Deadline during DrupalCon Chicago 2026.

## 4. Eligibility

### 4.1 Age and Residency

To be eligible, entrants must be legal residents of a jurisdiction where participation is lawful and must be at least 18 years of age (or the age of majority in their jurisdiction, whichever is greater) at the time of entry. The contest is void where prohibited or restricted by applicable law.

### 4.2 Employee and Affiliate Exclusion

Employees, officers, directors, and contractors of Acquia Inc. and its parent companies, subsidiaries, affiliates are not eligible to win prizes. Immediate family members are also not eligible to win prizes. Ineligible persons may submit showcase entries for recognition purposes only.

### 4.3 Team Participation and On-Site Requirement

Individuals or teams may participate. Remote collaborators may participate as part of a team. To be eligible for judging and prizes, each submission must identify one team lead who is physically attending DrupalCon Chicago 2026 on the entrant's own initiative and expense. DrupalCon Chicago 2026 is a separate, independently operated event; Acquia does not require or sponsor any participant's attendance at DrupalCon as a condition imposed by Acquia. The on-site requirement is an event participation structure, not a purchase required by Acquia.

The on-site team lead must be the primary contact for the submission and must be available in person on Wednesday afternoon for winner verification, judge follow-up, and prize coordination if needed. Teams without an on-site DrupalCon team lead may submit showcase entries, which will be publicly displayed and recognized but will not be scored or considered for prizes. Showcase entries are still subject to all submission conditions in Section 6. Each individual may be associated with only one prize-eligible entry.

## 5. How to Enter

To enter, participants must submit their work through the event repository using either of the following methods:

- A GitHub issue in this repository
- A pull request against this repository

Each submission must include the materials described in the landing page and starter kit, including:

- A meaningful agent-work outcome
- At least one AX artifact
- A README
- An Agent Run Log
- An Agent Experience Report
- The name or GitHub handle of the on-site DrupalCon team lead (for prize eligibility)

Incomplete, late, corrupted, inaccessible, or materially non-responsive submissions may be disqualified at Acquia's discretion. Submissions made through GitHub issues or pull requests will be publicly viewable. Entrants must not include secrets, credentials, or confidential information in submission materials.

## 6. Submission Conditions

Entrants may submit only work they have the right to share. Each entrant or team represents and warrants that:

- The submission is their own work product or includes materials they are authorized to use;
- The submission does not knowingly include confidential information they are not allowed to disclose;
- The submission does not violate the intellectual property, privacy, publicity, or other rights of any person or organization;
- The submission can be reviewed, discussed, and demonstrated publicly if selected as a winner or finalist.

For entries seeking the Best Contribution Back to Drupal prize category:

- The entrant must have the right to contribute the relevant materials back to Drupal or another intended open source target;
- Any Drupal-derivative code intended for Drupal.org-hosted projects must be capable of being released under Drupal-compatible licensing, which for such code generally means GPL-2.0-or-later;
- Any third-party code or assets that affect contribution eligibility must be identified by the entrant.

Each entrant or team agrees to defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Acquia, its affiliates, and event organizers from any third-party claims, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses arising from or related to: (a) a breach of the representations made in this section; (b) alleged or actual infringement, misappropriation, or violation of intellectual property, privacy, publicity, or other rights caused by the submission or its distribution; or (c) third-party claims tied to code, content, media, or other materials included in the submission, including materials generated in whole or in part by AI tools used by the entrant. This indemnification does not apply to claims arising from Acquia's own gross negligence or willful misconduct.

## 7. Judging

Eligible entries will be evaluated by Acquia and Drupal community judges using the published rubric in [`judging-rubric.md`](./judging-rubric.md) (the version published at the time of contest open governs). The judging framework considers the following criteria:

- Agent Success
- AX Quality
- Drupal-in-the-Loop
- Openness
- Impact

Judges may use AI-assisted summaries to help compare entries, but all final scoring and selection decisions are made by human judges. The tie-breaker order described in [`judging-rubric.md`](./judging-rubric.md) is incorporated into these rules by reference. Judges' decisions are final and binding on all matters relating to judging and scoring.

### 7.1 Conflict of Interest

Any judge who has a personal or professional interest in a submitted entry, including entries submitted by a team member, direct employer, client, or close associate, must disclose that interest to the contest administrator and recuse themselves from scoring that entry. Acquia reserves the right to substitute judges if a conflict cannot be resolved through recusal or if a judge becomes unavailable.

## 8. Prizes

There will be three (3) prize categories. The approximate retail value (ARV) and description of each prize is as follows:

- Best Overall: ARV $1,000 USD
- Best Agent: ARV $1,000 USD
- Best Contribution Back to Drupal: ARV $1,000 USD

Total ARV of all prizes: $3,000 USD. Each winning entry may receive only one prize. If a winning submission is created by a team, the prize will be coordinated through the named on-site team lead on behalf of the team. Team members are solely responsible for deciding how to divide the prize among themselves; Acquia has no responsibility or liability for any internal team dispute regarding prize allocation.

Sponsor may fulfill prizes via Venmo, check, ACH transfer, or another commercially reasonable payment method, and reserves the right to substitute an alternate fulfillment method for operational, compliance, or legal reasons. Prizes have no cash equivalent except as determined by Acquia at its sole discretion.

Prize fulfillment will be completed within sixty (60) days of winner confirmation, subject to receipt of any required tax documentation described in Section 11.

## 9. Winner Notification and Claim

Potential winners will be selected after judging is complete. Before being confirmed as a winner, a selected entrant may be required to verify eligibility, identity, on-site attendance, submission ownership, and any information required for prize administration.

Potential winners will be notified via the contact information associated with their submission. A potential winner must respond to the notification within five (5) business days of the notification being sent. If a selected winner cannot be contacted, does not respond within five (5) business days, cannot confirm eligibility, or cannot accept the prize for any reason, the prize may be forfeited and an alternate winner may be selected from remaining eligible entries.

Public winner announcements will occur only after the applicable winner has been confirmed and eligibility verified.
